Technical SEO Checklist

By Jan Christopher Buen • March 12, 2026 • 6 min read

1. Crawlability

Check robots.txt rules, noindex directives, canonical targets, and internal link access to important pages. If a crawler cannot discover a URL consistently, later optimizations matter less. The robots.txt Validator and XML Sitemap Validator are useful quick checks before a deeper crawl.

2. Indexation

Compare live URLs against XML sitemaps and search console data. Look for orphan pages, duplicate variations, and pages that should be indexed but lack strong internal paths.

3. Metadata and markup

Review title tags, meta descriptions, heading hierarchy, canonical tags, Open Graph tags, and structured data. Templates should handle these consistently. The Meta Tag Analyzer and Schema Markup Generator help speed up that review.

4. Architecture and performance

Reduce unnecessary depth, tighten internal linking, and evaluate render-blocking assets. Crawl efficiency and user experience often improve together when the structure is simplified. For a full example, see the Books to Scrape Technical SEO Audit.
