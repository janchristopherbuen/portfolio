Technical SEO analysis of the website's internal structure, crawl depth, and internal linking architecture.

Project Overview

This audit focused on how the Books to Scrape website organizes its pages, distributes internal links, and keeps important URLs within a reasonable crawl depth. The review looked at whether the site structure supports efficient discovery and a clear content hierarchy.

Objectives

review site directory structure
evaluate crawl tree hierarchy
measure crawl depth distribution
assess internal linking flow

Work Completed

  • Crawled the website to map the URL structure and identify the main content directories.
  • Reviewed the crawl tree graph to understand how the homepage, category pages, and product pages connect.
  • Measured crawl depth distribution to confirm whether important content remained accessible within a practical number of clicks.
  • Assessed internal linking flow to determine how well category pages support deeper discovery.
  • Summarized strengths and risks in the current architecture with practical maintenance recommendations.

Key SEO Findings

  • The website uses a clear directory-based structure, with the catalogue section holding the main commercial content.
  • The crawl tree follows a logical pattern from homepage to category pages and then to product pages, which supports discovery.
  • Most URLs sit within crawl depth level 2, keeping important content relatively close to the homepage.
  • Category pages act as effective internal-link hubs that help distribute relevance to product pages.
  • Deeper URLs could still benefit from ongoing internal-link review if the site expands or adds new sections.

Outcome

The audit showed a solid architecture foundation with a logical hierarchy, efficient crawl depth, and useful internal-link hubs through category pages. The main takeaway was to preserve the current structure while monitoring deeper-page visibility as the site grows.
