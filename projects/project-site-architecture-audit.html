Site Architecture Audit

Restructuring for Success: Optimal Internal Linking & Crawl Paths

Site Structure Internal Linking +25% Organic Growth

Project Overview

This site architecture audit reviewed a content-heavy website with over 2,000 pages across multiple topic categories. The site had grown organically without strategic structure planning, resulting in orphan pages, poor internal linking, and inefficient crawl paths.

The goal was to redesign the information architecture to improve crawl efficiency, establish clear link equity flow, and ensure all valuable pages were reachable through logical internal linking. Implementation took place over 10 weeks.

Problems Identified

Orphan Pages & Poor Discoverability

127 pages had no incoming internal links, making them invisible to search engines and invisible in crawl analysis. These pages received minimal organic traffic.

Deep Nesting & Crawl Waste

Some content pages required 8+ clicks from homepage, wasting crawl budget on deep pages while higher-priority pages weren't being crawled efficiently.

JavaScript Pagination Issues

Category pages used JavaScript pagination without HTML links, preventing search engines from crawling additional pages in pagination.

Duplicate Content & Parameter Confusion

Inconsistent URL parameters created multiple versions of same content, diluting link equity and confusing search engines about canonical versions.

Missing Internal Cross-Links

No topical clustering or contextual linking between related pages, missing opportunities to establish topical authority and improve page rankings.

Architecture Optimization Strategy

Analysis & Planning

  • ✓ Map entire site structure with Sitebulb
